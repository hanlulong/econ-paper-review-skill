# Contributing

Bug reports and feature requests are welcome through GitHub Issues. Opening an
issue or participating in a discussion does not require accepting the
Contributor License Agreement. Do not post a confidential manuscript or
third-party material that you are not authorized to share.

Specific code, text, prompts, examples, or patches offered in an issue will not
be incorporated into the project until they are moved to a pull request with
the required acknowledgement or covered by explicit email acceptance.

Contributors keep ownership of their work. Because Econ Paper Review may be
offered under both its public noncommercial licence and separate commercial
terms, source-code, documentation, design, prompt, schema, and test
contributions submitted for inclusion in the project are accepted under the
[Contributor License Agreement](docs/CONTRIBUTOR_LICENSE_AGREEMENT.md). An
accepted contribution remains available in the public version under the
licence that applied when it was submitted, while the agreement also permits
its use in commercial and hosted versions.

For most contributors, acceptance is one checkbox in the pull-request
template. Before opening a pull request:

1. Read the Contributor License Agreement.
2. Confirm that you own the contribution or have written authorization from
   its owner. Identify third-party material before submitting it and wait for
   written approval.
3. Keep the acknowledgement in the pull-request template and check its box.

The required acknowledgement identifies the frozen agreement version:

> I have read and agree to the Econ Paper Review Contributor License Agreement,
> Version 1.0 (13 July 2026). I am authorized to submit this contribution and
> grant the rights described there.

The checked pull-request acknowledgement, together with the GitHub record,
constitutes electronic acceptance. Maintainers must verify and retain that
record before merging. Version 1.0 is not changed retroactively; any future
terms will use a new numbered version so that the accepted terms remain
identifiable in repository history. If an employer, university, or another
entity owns the contribution, an authorized representative must also comment
with the entity's legal name, their name and title, and confirmation that they
accept the agreement for that entity.

For questions or an email-based acceptance record, contact
`hanlulong@gmail.com` before submitting the contribution.
