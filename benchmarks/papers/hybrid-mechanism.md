# Targets, Effort, and Sales

## Model

A worker privately chooses effort e. A public target raises the marginal return to effort. Proposition 1 shows that, holding productivity fixed, the target increases effort. The model does not contain sales, customer arrival, or reporting behavior.

## Experiment

We randomize 180 stores to receive a target poster or no poster. The pre-analysis plan names employee effort, but the dataset contains no effort measure. We inspect twelve sales, attendance, and survey outcomes and feature the three with p-values below 0.05. The paper reports conventional outcome-by-outcome p-values.

## Interpretation

Poster stores report higher sales and manager satisfaction. We conclude that the evidence confirms the model's effort mechanism. Customer demand, manager reporting, and employee attendance could also change sales, but these alternatives are not compared.
