# Targets, Effort, and Sales

## Model

A worker privately chooses effort e. A public target raises the marginal return to effort. Proposition 1 shows that, holding productivity fixed, the target increases effort. The paper's motivating claim is that targets improve store performance because they change worker effort, but the model does not contain sales, customer arrival, or reporting behavior and supplies no mapping from effort to the reported performance outcome.

## Experiment

We randomize 180 stores to receive a target poster or no poster. The poster is installed at the store entrance, where both employees and customers can see it. The pre-analysis plan names employee effort, but the dataset contains no effort measure. We inspect twelve sales, attendance, and survey outcomes and feature the three with p-values below 0.05. The paper reports conventional outcome-by-outcome p-values.

## Interpretation

Poster stores report higher sales and manager satisfaction. We conclude that the evidence confirms the model's effort mechanism. Yet the visible poster could change customer demand directly, bypassing worker effort; manager reporting and employee attendance could also change sales. These alternatives are not compared.
