# Entry Costs and Retail Variety

## Model and estimation

Retailers choose entry and price in differentiated local markets. Demand curvature is governed by eta and the fixed entry cost by f. We calibrate both eta and f so that the model matches the observed mean number of firms. No other targeted moment varies separately with either parameter. The paper describes both parameters as identified by the data.

## Computation

For each market, we solve the equilibrium from one initial price vector and stop when the maximum price change is below 0.01. We do not report alternative starting values or tighter tolerances. All reported markets converge under this rule.

## Counterfactual

We remove half of the licensing fee and hold eta, f, demand shifters, and the distribution of potential entrants fixed. The model predicts a 14 percent increase in variety and a welfare gain of 2.1 percent. We interpret this as the causal welfare effect of the policy in any retail market.
