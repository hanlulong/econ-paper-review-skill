# Entry Costs and Retail Variety

## Question and promised objects

The paper asks how licensing fees affect retail variety and welfare. It also promises counterfactual results for the price distribution and a decomposition of welfare into consumer surplus, fee revenue, and entry costs.

## Model and estimation

Retailers choose entry and price in differentiated local markets. Demand curvature is governed by eta and the fixed entry cost by f. We calibrate both eta and f so that the model matches the observed mean number of firms. No other targeted moment varies separately with either parameter. The paper describes both parameters as identified by the data.

## Computation

For each market, we solve the equilibrium from one initial price vector and stop when the maximum price change is below 0.01. We do not report alternative starting values or tighter tolerances. All reported markets converge under this rule.

## Model fit

The model exactly matches the targeted mean number of firms. Its average price is 18 percent above the corresponding data moment, but the paper states that the model reproduces both entry and price facts closely. No other fit or validation evidence is reported before the counterfactual.

## Counterfactual

We remove half of the licensing fee and hold eta, f, demand shifters, compliance, and the distribution of potential entrants fixed. The policy is therefore allowed to change only the fee paid by firms already represented in the model; changes in application, compliance, or the pool of potential entrants are ruled out without discussion. The model predicts a 14 percent increase in variety and a welfare gain of 2.1 percent. The paper does not define whether 2.1 percent is a level, a consumption-equivalent change, or a change relative to baseline welfare, and it does not say whether lost fee revenue is a transfer or a resource saving. The promised price-distribution result and welfare decomposition are not reported. We interpret the two headline numbers as the causal effects of the policy in any retail market.
