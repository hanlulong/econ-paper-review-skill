# Reminders and Worker Saving

## Abstract

We randomly assigned 240 volunteers at one factory to receive a weekly saving reminder or no reminder. The reminder increased reported saving after one week by 9 percentage points. We conclude that reminders increase saving for all manufacturing workers.

## Design and measurement

Assignment was individual and generated independently. Saving was self-reported in a follow-up survey. We removed the top and bottom 5 percent of saving within each assignment arm to reduce measurement error. Of 120 assigned participants per arm, 103 treated and 78 control participants completed follow-up. We analyze respondents and do not adjust for nonresponse because treatment was randomized.

## Results

The difference in mean saving among retained respondents is 9 percentage points. Conventional heteroskedasticity-robust standard errors give a p-value of 0.04. The result is robust to a Huber regression run on the same trimmed respondent sample.

## Conclusion

The experiment shows that low-cost reminders raise saving for workers generally.
