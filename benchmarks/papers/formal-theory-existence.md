# Choice with Costly Delay

## Environment

An agent chooses x in the open interval (0,1). Utility is u(x)=x. Proposition 1 states that a unique optimal choice exists and equals x=1. The proof notes that utility is strictly increasing and therefore takes its highest value at the upper boundary.

## Coordination extension

Two agents simultaneously choose Left or Right. Both receive one when their choices match and zero otherwise. Proposition 2 states that the game has a unique equilibrium in which both choose Right. No refinement or tie-breaking assumption is imposed.

## Interpretation

The conclusion states that the propositions show delayed choice always produces a unique efficient outcome in all markets, including environments with uncertainty and private information. Those environments are not part of the model.
