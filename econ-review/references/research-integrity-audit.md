# Conditional Research Integrity, Ethics, and Data-Governance Audit

Load this protocol only when facts in the manuscript or supplied materials create a research-integrity, participant-protection, disclosure, or data-governance burden. It is a disclosure and claim-calibration audit, not legal advice, an institutional ethics review, or a misconduct screen.

## Activate from facts, not from a universal checklist

Consider the burden when the work involves one or more of the following:

- human participants, field interventions, surveys, experiments, deception, sensitive topics, or material participant risk;
- personal, confidential, restricted, proprietary, linked, geocoded, or platform data;
- prospective trials or registrations that the manuscript claims govern the analysis;
- third-party data, software, images, instruments, or records whose access or reuse rights affect publication or replication;
- funding, sponsor involvement, conflicts, or researcher roles that may affect design, analysis, interpretation, or required disclosure;
- animal subjects or another regulated research object explicitly present in the work.

Do not activate a complaint merely because an approval, consent statement, registration, license, conflict declaration, or funding statement is absent from the excerpt currently being read. Search the full manuscript, title material, notes, appendix, registration links, data statement, and supplied documentation first.

## Reconstruct the relevant governance object

Record only what is needed to evaluate the paper:

- activity, population, intervention or data class that creates the burden;
- approval, exemption, consent or waiver, deception and debriefing, registration, access agreement, or disclosure claimed by the paper;
- institution, jurisdiction, venue, registry, or data-provider rule only when it is known and relevant;
- timing relative to data collection or intervention;
- confidentiality, minimization, security, linkage, retention, sharing, and disclosure constraints that affect what can responsibly appear in the paper or package;
- sponsor or researcher control over design, data, analysis, publication, and interpretation when the manuscript makes that relevant.

Never request participant identities, protected records, credentials, contracts, approval letters, or other confidential material for the review artifact. A manuscript statement, redacted documentation, stable registry record, or bounded explanation may be sufficient.

## Evaluate proportionately

Distinguish:

- `documented_and_claim_bounded`: the relevant safeguard or restriction is disclosed and the paper's claims and replication promises respect it;
- `disclosure_needed`: a missing statement prevents a reader or editor from understanding participant protection, registration status, data rights, sponsor role, or lawful/authorized reproducibility;
- `claim_or_package_exceeds_governance`: the manuscript promises sharing, reuse, linkage, or implementation beyond the stated permission or governance boundary;
- `bounded`: the applicable rule, approval status, rights, or confidential documentation cannot be established from authorized materials;
- `not_applicable`: no source-derived trigger remains after the scoped search.

Do not infer fabrication, unethical conduct, illegality, or intent from missing prose, inaccessible documentation, a registration deviation, or an apparent governance conflict. Describe the observable disclosure or scope problem and what evidence would resolve it. Verify any venue, registry, provider, or jurisdiction-specific requirement from a current official source before calling it required; otherwise state the general transparency issue and mark specific compliance bounded.

Registration is not a universal requirement for every design. When a registration or analysis plan exists, reconcile dates, outcomes, estimands, exclusions, and declared deviations only to the extent that they bear on the paper's claims. Transparent deviations are not automatically defects.

Restricted or nonshareable data are not a criticism when the restriction is genuine, disclosed, and the reproducibility claim stays inside it. Ask for a usable access description, synthetic or nonconfidential scaffolding, or claim narrowing only when feasible and decision-relevant.

## Finding and reporting rules

Create an author-facing finding only when the issue is evidenced, material to evaluation or responsible dissemination, and repairable through a proportionate disclosure, correction, governance-aligned package change, claim boundary, or documented approval/registration clarification. Route ordinary disclosure mechanics to writing; route threats to study interpretation, selective-reporting claims, participant safety, data rights, or reproducibility to substance.

Keep sensitive concerns concise and private. If supplied evidence indicates an acute participant, privacy, or unauthorized-disclosure risk, stop the risky operation, preserve no unnecessary sensitive content in the review, and tell the user what boundary prevents continuation. Do not conduct an external investigation or contact a journal, institution, registry, data provider, participant, or author without explicit authority.

Record the activated burden, source trigger, scope checked, status, official source if used, finding IDs, and the least intrusive evidence that would resolve a bounded state. A clean state is a safeguard worth preserving; it is not a reason to add boilerplate praise.
